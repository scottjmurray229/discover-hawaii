## Discovering Lanai

Lanai is the smallest publicly accessible Hawaiian island and the most disconnected from the Hawaii that most tourists experience. There are no traffic lights. No chain restaurants. No high-rise hotels. The island was a Dole pineapple plantation for most of the 20th century and today is 98% owned by Larry Ellison, who has invested heavily in making it a wellness and luxury destination while the broader island remains largely undeveloped.

Hulopo'e Bay at Manele is one of the finest beaches in Hawaii — crescent white sand, spinner dolphins in the bay most mornings, and snorkeling so clear over the reef it feels like aquarium glass. The Garden of the Gods is a high-desert landscape of eroded red and orange boulders that looks nothing like the rest of Hawaii. Shipwreck Beach, reached by 4WD on red clay roads along the northeast coast, has the hull of a WWII liberty ship rusting into the reef in the shallows.

A day trip from Maui on the Expeditions Ferry ($30 each way) is the most efficient way to experience Lanai without the Four Seasons price tag.
